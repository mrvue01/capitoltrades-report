# AI Use Policy Template

> Customize this template for each client. This is not legal advice.

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how employees may use artificial intelligence tools in a way that supports productivity while protecting company information, client confidentiality, and work quality.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and temporary staff who use AI tools in connection with company work.

## 3. Approved AI tools

Employees may only use AI tools that have been approved by the company.

Approved tools:
- [List approved tools]

Unapproved tools may not be used for company work unless written approval is provided.

## 4. Allowed uses

Approved uses may include:
- drafting internal notes
- summarizing non-sensitive content
- brainstorming ideas
- improving grammar or tone
- drafting first-pass marketing content
- internal research support

## 5. Prohibited uses

Employees may not use AI tools to:
- enter confidential client data into unapproved tools
- enter regulated, sensitive, or protected information unless explicitly allowed
- make final legal, financial, medical, HR, or compliance decisions without human review
- represent AI-generated output as verified fact without checking it
- send AI-generated client communications without appropriate review
- upload contracts, employee records, financial statements, or customer records into unapproved systems

## 6. Data handling rules

Employees must not enter the following into unapproved AI tools:
- client confidential information
- personally identifiable information
- employee records
- financial records
- legal documents
- protected health information
- internal credentials, secrets, or sensitive company plans

When in doubt, employees must assume the information is not safe to enter and ask a manager first.

## 7. Human review requirement

All AI-generated output must be reviewed by a responsible human before being:
- sent to clients
- published externally
- used for decision-making
- included in contracts, reports, or recommendations

The employee using the AI tool remains responsible for the final content.

## 8. Accuracy and verification

AI tools can generate incorrect, incomplete, or misleading content. Employees must verify important facts, calculations, citations, summaries, and recommendations before use.

## 9. Ownership and accountability

Management will assign an internal owner for AI governance. That person is responsible for:
- maintaining the approved tools list
- reviewing policy updates
- handling questions or exceptions
- coordinating future staff training

Internal owner:
- [Name / title]

## 10. Training

Employees who use AI tools for work must complete required internal guidance or training before using approved tools for business purposes.

## 11. Violations

Violations of this policy may result in loss of tool access, corrective action, or other disciplinary measures consistent with company policy.

## 12. Review cycle

This policy should be reviewed every 90 to 180 days, or sooner if the company adopts new tools, new workflows, or new legal/compliance requirements.
