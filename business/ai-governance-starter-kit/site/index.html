The problem is not AI. The problem is unmanaged AI.

Most small businesses are already experimenting with AI, but very few have written rules, approval paths, or practical guardrails. That leaves owners exposed to data leakage, inconsistent quality, and compliance trouble they did not mean to create.

Data exposure

Staff enter the wrong information

Client records, contracts, HR details, and internal documents can end up inside tools that were never approved for that kind of data.

Output risk

AI answers get treated like facts

Without human review rules, teams can send polished but inaccurate work to clients, managers, or the public.

Oversight gap

No one owns the process

New tools appear, employees improvise, and leadership has no clean view of what is allowed, restricted, or prohibited.
