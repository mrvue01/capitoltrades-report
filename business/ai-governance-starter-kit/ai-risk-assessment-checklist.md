# AI Risk Assessment Checklist

Use this checklist during client discovery and delivery.

## 1. Inventory current AI use
- [ ] Identify all AI tools currently in use
- [ ] Identify unofficial or personal-account use
- [ ] Identify departments using AI
- [ ] Identify common AI-assisted workflows
- [ ] Identify client-facing use cases

## 2. Review data handling risk
- [ ] Determine whether staff enter confidential business data into AI tools
- [ ] Determine whether client data is entered into AI tools
- [ ] Determine whether employee or HR data is entered into AI tools
- [ ] Determine whether financial, legal, or regulated data is entered into AI tools
- [ ] Determine whether there are data classification rules today

## 3. Review policy gap
- [ ] Confirm whether a written AI policy exists
- [ ] Confirm whether tool approval rules exist
- [ ] Confirm whether prohibited uses are defined
- [ ] Confirm whether human review requirements are defined
- [ ] Confirm whether managers know how AI is currently used

## 4. Review quality and oversight risk
- [ ] Check whether AI-generated content is reviewed before external use
- [ ] Check whether factual verification rules exist
- [ ] Check whether AI can be used in sensitive decisions
- [ ] Check whether client communications can be AI-assisted without review
- [ ] Check whether employees understand hallucination risk

## 5. Review legal and compliance considerations
- [ ] Identify any regulated or contract-sensitive data
- [ ] Identify privacy obligations
- [ ] Identify confidentiality obligations
- [ ] Identify recordkeeping concerns
- [ ] Identify whether external legal/compliance review may be needed

## 6. Review rollout readiness
- [ ] Confirm executive sponsor
- [ ] Confirm policy owner
- [ ] Confirm staff training audience
- [ ] Confirm review cycle for future updates
- [ ] Confirm whether ongoing support is desired

## 7. Assign overall maturity

### AI governance maturity rating
- [ ] Level 1: Unmanaged
- [ ] Level 2: Informal use with little oversight
- [ ] Level 3: Basic rules in place
- [ ] Level 4: Structured governance and review
- [ ] Level 5: Mature and regularly reviewed

## 8. Final output summary
- [ ] Top 3 risks identified
- [ ] Top 3 quick wins
- [ ] Policy complexity level needed
- [ ] Tools matrix needed
- [ ] Training priority areas
- [ ] Quarterly review recommended
